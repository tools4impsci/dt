The Design Thinking Landscape

Why the Triple Diamond Framework is Essential for Social Impact

Introduction

Developed by the UK Design Council, an evolution of the Double Diamond (Discover, Define, Develop, Deliver/Sustain).

Why "Triple"?

Complex social problems are "wicked problems"â€”ill-defined, interconnected, and resistant to simple solutions. The third diamond is crucial to ensure the solution is equitable, ethical, and sustainable.

Social Impact Goal

The framework provides a structured approach to move from ambiguity to actionable insights to sustainable change.

Foundational Principles of HCD

1.

Empathy

Deep understanding of the community, context, power dynamics, and historical trauma. Moving beyond "user needs" to community needs.

2.

Collaboration

Co-design with affected stakeholders, bringing together diverse perspectives (e.g., policy, non-profit, community members).

3.

Iteration

Embrace failure as learning. Avoid implementing harmful solutions in fragile community settings. Test early and often.

4.

User-Centeredness

Focus on the strengths and existing capacities of the community, not just their deficits. This is an asset-based approach.

Diamond 1 - Discover

Divergence: Research, Immersion, and Uncovering Root Causes

Key Methods & Activities

  • Contextual Inquiry: Spend time where the problem exists, observe, and engage.
  • Journey Mapping: Map the current experience to identify pain points.
  • System Mapping: Analyze the broader ecosystem to understand systemic failures.
  • Key Informant Interviews: Hear first-hand accounts and perspectives.

Output/Deliverable

  • Comprehensive Problem & User Ecosystem Maps.
  • Affinity Diagrams and data synthesis.
  • Initial Problem Hypotheses.

Diamond 2 - Define

Convergence: Synthesizing Insights and Defining a Focused Problem

Key Methods & Activities

  • Persona Creation: Develop rich profiles to humanize the data.
  • "How Might We" Workshops: Translate insights into an actionable HMW question.
  • Ideation: Brainstorm a broad range of potential solutions.
  • Storyboarding: Visualize the future solution in action.

Output/Deliverable

  • Community Personas and Journey Maps.
  • The "How Might We" (HMW) statement.
  • Clear Success Metrics defined with the community.

Diamond 3 - Develop & Sustain

Ideation, Experimentation, and Ethical Sustainment

Key Methods & Activities

  • Rapid Prototyping: Build lo-fi versions of the solution (e.g., paper mockups).
  • User Testing: Gather feedback on prototypes to refine the design.
  • Feasibility & Viability Workshops: Ensure the solution is viable.
  • Implementation & Scale: Plan for phased rollout and sustainable funding.

Output/Deliverable

  • A funnel of Conceptual Solutions.
  • Validated Minimum Viable Product (MVP).
  • A Governance Model (often community-led).
  • A Sustainable Funding Model.

Critical Reflection

Practicing Design Thinking as Systemic Change

The Triple Diamond is not a linear checklist; it is an adaptive mindset that forces designers to embrace ambiguity and commit to continuous learning and co-creation.

The quality of the solution is directly tied to the depth of the initial empathy and collaboration.
