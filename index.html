The iSense Project Timeline

IDT x BCSSW

This project visualizes the six-week process for undergraduate teams tackling the "iSense challenge." The workflow strictly follows the five phases of Design Thinking, progressing from deep user understanding to tangible prototyping and a final presentation.

Project Phases & Duration

The core project timeline is organized by Design Thinking phases, showing exact week numbers and the associated dates for each activity. The timeline progresses chronologically from bottom to top.

Design Thinking Phases in Detail

Week 1 (Oct 30)

Phase 1: Empathize / Discover

The initial step focuses on deep user immersion and understanding the patient's world. Teams work to step into the user's shoes and collect rich qualitative data.

Key Activities

  • Human Ecosystem Mapping
  • Conducting User Interviews
  • Initial Problem Scoping

Week 2 (Nov 6)

Phase 2: Define

This phase synthesizes the data collected. Teams shift from raw observations to creating a clear, actionable problem statement based on user needs.

Key Deliverables

  • Patient Journey Mapping
  • Refined Point of View (POV) Statement
  • 2-3 "How Might We..." Questions

Week 3 (Nov 13)

Phase 3: Ideation

A period of Divergent Thinking, generating a large quantity of diverse and creative solution ideas to the defined problem statement before converging on a single concept.

Key Activities

  • Brainstorming by Sense/SCAMPER
  • Dot Voting/Concept Convergence
  • 6-Panel Storyboarding of Final Concept

Week 4 (Nov 20)

Phase 4: Prototype

Ideas are made tangible through a Maker Session. The focus is on low-fidelity prototypes designed for rapid learning, not polished final products.

Key Deliverables

  • Low-Fidelity Physical Prototype
  • Service Prototype (Role-playing)
  • Initial Presentation Materials

Weeks 5-6 (Nov 21 - Dec 4)

Phase 5: Test & Pitch

The final phase involves structured peer-to-peer testing, iterative refinement, and culminating in the final Pitch-A-Thon presentation to the stakeholders.

Key Activities

  • "I Like / I Wish / What If" Feedback
  • Final Prototype Refinement
  • 3-Minute Pitch Presentation (Pitch-A-Thon)
